Balbacua (The mix of spices and seasonings blends perfectly with the tenderness of the beef. Each serving indulges diners with heritage Filipino flavors.)

Etymology. The name balbacua is derived from the Latin American dish barbacoa (which is also the source of the English word "barbecue"), though they are very different dishes. While balbacua is a beef stew, barbacoa is instead meat roasted in a pit.

    Ingridients:
  • Ginger, chopped 31 g
  • Red Onions, chopped 200 g
  • Smoked Sweet Paprika 10 g
  • Knorr Liquid Seasoning 3.8L 30 ml
  • Tomato Paste 40 g
  • Lemongras, pounded and tied to a knot 2 pc
  • Crushed Black Pepper, ground 2 g
  • Bay Leaf 1 pc
  • Pork and Beans, canned 390 g
  • Green Chillies, whole 2 pc
  • Black Beans, washed 100 g
  • Onion Leeks, chopped 50 g, Knorr Rostip Chicken Seasoning Powder 1kg 5 g

Meat Preparation

Procedure
  • Beef Face/Skin: cleaned, boiled for 5 minutes (Liquid discarded), cut in large cubes 1.25 kg Beef Feet: cleaned, boiled for 5 minutes (Liquid discarded), cut in large cubes 1.25 kg Water 3 l
  • Preparation Meat Preparation Place everything in a Pressure Cooker. Once it whistles, let it cook for about 40 min. Check if the meats are already slightly tender since you will still further cook it as a stew. Strain the meat and reserve the Stock. Set aside. Sauce Procedure In a separate pot, heat the anato oil and saute garlic, ginger, red onions until cook and fragrant. Add now the strained beef. Saute then add paprika, Knorr Liquid seasoning and tomato paste. Keep on sauteing until well incorporated in medium heat to avoid burning. Meal Procedure Add the rest of the ingredients, mix well and bring to a boil then simmer for another 30- 40min or until the meats are tender. To finish, add onion leaks and adjsut taste by adding Knorr Rostip.

BALBACUA is a beef stew dish. It is made with beef skin, oxtail, and some spices cooked for several hours or till tender. This dish is originated from Visayan Regions of the Visayas and Mindanao islands, each province has its own twist in cooking this dish. I hope it helps you out.

Kare-Kare (Kare-Kare is a type of Filipino stew with a rich and thick peanut sauce.)

Kare Kare is a type of Filipino stew with a rich and thick peanut sauce. It is a popular dish in the Philippines served during special occasions. The traditional recipe is composed of ox tail. There are instances wherein both ox tripe and tail are used. The vegetable components of the dish are string beans, eggplant, bok choy, and banana blossoms. Lightly browned toasted ground rice is used to thicken the sauce.

    Ingridients:
  • 2 lbs. oxtail pressure cooked for 30 minutes
  • ½ cup ground peanuts
  • ¼ cup peanut butter
  • ½ cup annatto seeds diluted in ½ cup warm water
  • 2 1/2 cups beef broth
  • 1 medium onion minced
  • 3 cloves garlic crushed and minced
  • 1 tablespoon glutinous rice powder
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 2 sliced Chinese eggplant steamed
  • 1 bunch bok choy steamed
  • 1 bunch string beans cut into short pieces steamed
  • ½ cup shrimp paste bagoong, cooked

Traditional way of cooking

Procedure
    Simmer oxtail in water. Drain and discard the water. Simmer blanched oxtail in water until slightly tender (approximately 2 - 3 hours). Add bay leaves and black peppercorn and simmer until very tender. Remove oxtail and set aside. Strain oxtail stock and reserve 8 cups.

    180 Min

    While waiting for ox tail to tenderize, simmer tripe in water in another pot. Drain and discard the water. Repeat twice. Simmer blanched oxtail in water until tender. Drain and discard liquid.

    20 Min

    Sauté garlic and onion in oil in a pot. Add ground rice. Stir in oxtail stock. Slowly add annatto water until desired color is achieved. Stir in the ground peanut and bring to a simmer. Add ox tail and tripe. Season with MAGGI® Magic Sarap®.

    20 Min

    For the vegetables, blanch sitaw and pechay separately. Fry eggplant in oil until golden brown and set aside.

    5 Min

    Transfer kare-kare into a deep dish bowl, top with the vegetables and serve with bagoong. Palayok or clay pot is used to cook this dish. It also functions as the serving bowl at the same time.The best way to eat kare-kare is to pair it with plain white rice. Bagoong alamang or fermented shrimp paste completes the dish by providing flavor. Simply combine a spoonful of rice and kare-kare with a small portion of bagoong and enjoy!

There are several versions of kare-kare. The most common difference of each has something to do with the meat ingredient. We now know that the traditional protein used for this dish is oxtail. Tripe is also a good ingredient to use. There are also versions that make use of other types of meat, and even seafood.
